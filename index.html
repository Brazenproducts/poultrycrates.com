Three Use Cases. One Crate System.

Elipacko's poultry crate range covers all three stages of the supply chain:

Stage 1

Live Bird Transport

Ventilated walls with optimised perforation pattern maintain airflow around birds during transport. Meets EU Council Directive 93/119/EC and Regulation (EC) 1/2005 animal transport welfare standards. Perforations are sized to prevent wing and leg entrapment.

Stage 2

Processing Line Handling

After slaughter, solid-wall variants move processed product through the evisceration, chilling, and portioning stages. Fully radiused internal corners eliminate product trap points. Compatible with overhead conveyor, roller conveyor, and manual handling.

Stage 3

Cold Chain & Export

Solid-wall crates with lid seal product at the chiller before palletising for distribution or export. Blast-freeze rated to −30°C. ISPM 15 compliant — no wood content means no phytosanitary documentation for international shipments.

Plastic vs Wire Mesh Poultry Crates

Wire mesh crates have been the live transport standard for decades — but their operational disadvantages over a 3–5 year period consistently justify the switch to plastic for most operations above 50,000 birds per week.

Colour Coding for Multi-Species HACCP Plans

Operations processing both broilers and layers, or poultry alongside other species, typically require physical colour separation of crates between species or zones under their HACCP plan. Elipacko compounds colour at the resin level — the colour is structural, not a surface coat, and survives repeated high-pressure hot water cleaning indefinitely.

Standard HACCP colour options include white (processed product), yellow (live bird), red (reject / condemnation), blue (chiller), and green (despatch). Custom RAL colours are available for facilities with existing colour-coding systems.

Return Freight and Empty Storage

The 8:1 nesting ratio of Elipacko's live bird crates means a truck that delivers 800 loaded crates can return with all 800 empty crates in the space of 100 loaded crates. For operations running dedicated return trips, this alone reduces back-haul freight cost by 50–60% compared to wire crates with a 2:1 nest ratio. Contact Elipacko for bulk pricing and lead times.
